Projects

Research surrounding animal welfare solutions, specifically for the emotional welfare of livestock animals, has been largely unexplored. The few solutions that do exist are not standardized across the livestock industry, are inaccessible, and would not be appropriate for long-term use. This study attempts to bridge this gap by building and testing a 2D convolutional neural network that can categorize the body language of dairy cows, which prior research has found to be indicative of affective state. The network built by this study failed to reach the ~90% accuracy benchmark for emotion recognition in humans, and therefore would not currently be a feasible solution for emotional welfare monitoring in livestock. Future research should not focus on optimizing the model provided by this study, but rather on creating a new 3D model that would allow for the analysis of video footage and more accurate results.
